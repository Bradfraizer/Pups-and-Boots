In this example, I have two images taking up all twelve columns with an equal width (6). To position them in the middle, the first image needed to be pushed over three column slots with the col-[size]-push-3 class.

In this second example, I have two images taking up all twelve columns with an equal width (6), but in order allign them to the outer walls of their container the second image needed to be pushed over with the col-[size]-push-3 class.

In this third example, I am having fun with nesting column items. To achieve this, instead of creating sibling divs that house my images, I needed to make the back image div the parent to the second image. The first image recieved the col-[size]-12 class and col-[size]-pull-3 class to center it on the page. The second image was given the classes of col[size]-6 and col[size]-push-9 to nest it nicely in it's parent image.

In this fourth example, I have three pictures taking up all twelve columns with equal widths (4). The first image is pushed over with the col-[size]-push-3 class while the second image and third images are pushed towards the right side with the col-[size]-pull-1 class.

In this fifth example, I have three images of taking up equal sized columns (4) and are spaced evenly on the row. In order to achieve that, the second image needed to be pushed over one position and the third image pushed two. They were assigned the col-[size]-4 and col-[size]-push-* classes.

For example six, all three of the images are assigned the same column size (4) and are pulled over to the left margin of their ccontainer. To accomplish this, image two was given the col[size]-pull-2 class while the second image recieved col-[size]-pull-4.

In example seven, there are three images with equal width (4) all hugging the right side of the container. To achieve this, I needed to give image one the class of col-[size]-push-6, the second image col-[size]-push-4 and the third image col-[size]-push-2.

For example eigth, I have switched things up and added an additional image onto the row. To fit it in the row that contains only 12 columns, each image was given an equal column width of 3 (3×4). Only two of the images required pushing and those were the images three and four on the right side. To move them, I applied the col-[size]-push-1 class to them both.

As you can see in example nine, we are still using four images with an equal width (4) on the row. Images two and three are hugging the middle, while images one and four are at the corner of their container. To get the desired alignment, images two and four were given the col-[size]-push-1 class.

In example ten, we have twelve images of equal width (1) on the row, taking up ever every available column slot on the grid. No adjustments were neccessary.
